Onsen Tamago

★★

Lesley

Prep Time: 5 min Cook Time: 25 min Difficulty: Easy Servings: 4 Source: app.ckbk.com

Ingredients

4 eggs, at room temperature

200 ml (7 fl oz) soy sauce

3 rounded tablespoons katsuobushi**

3 cm x 3 cm (1¼ in x 1¼ in) piece kombu seaweed

Directions

Place all of the dashi shoyu ingredients in a jar and let them infuse overnight. If you don’t have time, you can bring the mixture to the boil in a saucepan, turn off the heat and let it infuse for 30 minutes. Dashi shoyu is a useful condiment for sashimi, salads and as a substitute for soy sauce. As it will keep for 1 month in the refrigerator, I recommend having some on hand all the time, ready to use. Place the eggs in a small, heavy pot or casserole dish with a well-fitting lid. Pour over enough boiling water to completely cover the eggs. Cover with a lid and leave for 18 minutes.

Break an egg into each individual bowl and pour over a tablespoon of dashi shoyu.
