Simmered Chicken with Vegetables (301 kcal / Serving)

Source: Japanese Cooking; Shizuo Tsuji

Ingredients

1 pound (450 g) boned chicken thighs, with skin

4-6 fresh shiitake mushrooms, wiped and trimmed

2 medium carrots

3-4 inches (8-10 cm) medium bamboo shoot, washed

1 medium lotus root, scrubbed with a brush

vegetable oil

1 cup dashi

2 Tbsps sugar

3 Tbsps light soy sauce

2 Tbsps dark soy sauce

2 ounces (60 g) snow peas, parboiled

Directions

To prepare: Cut the chicken into Â¾-inch (2-cm) cubes. Scrape, peel, or trim the vegetables as required and cut into rolling wedges (the ran-giri technique, see this page). Keep cut lotus root in water to avoid discoloration.

For excellence in flavor, parboil each of the vegetables separately in lightly salted water, rinse under cold water, and drain. The parboiling is optional.
To cook and serve: Heat 1 or 2 Tbsps oil (use as little oil as possible) in a medium-sized pot over high heat till very hot and add the chicken first, then the mushrooms, carrot, bamboo shoot, and root in that order. Stir-fry, using a wooden spoon, about 3 minutes, till all ingredients are well coated with hot oil and partially done.

Pour the dashi over the fried meat and vegetables to barely cover. Bring to a boil over high heat and add the sugar and soy sauces (if you do not have light soy sauce, use a total of 6 Tbsps of dark soy sauce). Cover with a drop-lid (otoshi-buta) or circle of baking paper with a vent (see this page) and simmer till reduced by approximately 30 percent. Remove from heat. Mix in parboiled snow peas for color just before serving.

Serve hot or at room temperature in individual bowls, making sure the variety of ingredients is attractively represented in each dish.
