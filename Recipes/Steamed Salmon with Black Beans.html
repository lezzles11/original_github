Steamed Salmon with Black Beans

★★★★

Lesley

Source: Ken Hom

Ingredients

450g skinless, boneless salmon, divided into 4 equal pieces

salt and white pepper

2 tbsp (30ml) black beans, rinsed and chopped

11/2 tbsp (22ml) finely chopped garlic

1 tbsp (15ml) finely chopped fresh ginger

1½ tbsp (22ml) Shaoxing rice wine or dry sherry

1 tbsp (15ml) light soy sauce

2 tbsp (30ml) light soy sauce

3 green onions, finely shredded a small handful of cilantro

1.5 tbsp (22ml) peanut or vegetable oil

Directions

1. Sprinkle the salmon pieces evenly with 1 tsp (sml) of salt and pepper to taste.

2. Combine the black beans, garlic and ginger in a small bowl. Put the salmon on a heatproof plate and evenly scatter the black bean mixture over the top.

3. Now pour the rice wine and soy sauce over the fish. Next, set up a steamer, or put a rack into a wok or deep saucepan, and fill it with 2 in (5cm) of water.

4. Bring the water to a boil over high heat. Carefully lower the fish, on its plate, into the steamer or onto the rack. Turn the heat to low and cover the wok or saucepan tightly.

5. Steam gently for 8-10 minutes, depending on the thickness of the fillets, replenishing the water in the steamer from time to time as necessary.

6. When the fish is cooked, remove from the wok and drain off any liquid. Pour over the soy sauce and scatter the green onions and cilantro on top of the fish.

7. Heat a small saucepan over high heat until it is hot. Add the oil, and when it is very hot and slightly smoking, pour it over the fillets. Serve at once.
