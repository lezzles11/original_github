Steamed Mussels with Ginger, Lemongrass and Coconut Milk

Difficulty: Easy Servings: 8 Source: Frank Dangereux

Ingredients

3 kg fresh mussels (for a starter portion; use 5 kg for a main course)

2 small onions, chopped

1 tablespoon garlic, chopped

2 tablespoons Tom Yum paste (paste made from crushed galanga, lemongrass and chilli. It can be bought in jars at supermarkets)

1 small root ginger, washed and thinly sliced with the skin on

3 mild red or green chillies, split in half

1 tin coconut milk (check on the label that this does not have added sugar)

½ bottle white wine

1 bunch fresh coriander, washed and coarsely chopped

canola oil for frying

Directions

Clean the mussels (see HOW TO). Heat a splash of oil in a large pot which has a fitted lid. Fry the onions until transparent.

Add the garlic, chillies and ginger and fry for about two minutes until these start to soften.

Add the Tom Yum paste and stir in well, until the mixture turns orange. It will start to stick slightly to the bottom of the pot.

Add the mussels, wine and coconut milk and place the lid on the pot. The mussels should not be more than three layers deep or they won’t open. You may have to use two pots or do the mussels in a few batches. If you do this, divide the ingredients between the pots/batches. Steam (boil) for 4-5 minutes on high heat. Remove from the heat and discard any mussels that have not opened.

Serve in large bowls with a good amount of juice poured over the mussels. Sprinkle with chopped coriander. Eat the mussels and drink all the juice – it is heavenly.
