Quick-Fried Beef with Spinach

Difficulty: Easy Source: Keith Floyd

Ingredients

500 g/1 lb 2 oz piece fillet steak, membrane trimmed off

5 cm /2 inch piece fresh root ginger, peeled and cut into very thin slices

100 ml/3½ fl oz fresh chicken stock (or use ½ stock cube dissolved in the same amount of water)

2 bags of baby spinach leaves, washed and dried

vegetable oil, for stir-frying

FOR THE MARINADE:

2 tablespoons dark soy sauce

2 or 3 tablespoons rice wine, sake or dry sherry

1 tsp sugar

½ tsp cornflour mixed with a little cold water to the consistency of single cream

a dash of sesame oil

Directions

Place the fillet steak in the freezer for 1 hour as this will make it easier to slice. Meanwhile, mix all the ingredients for the marinade in a bowl.

Remove the beef from the freezer and cut into wafer-thin slices - once sliced it will quickly defrost. Place the slices in the marinade and turn to coat the meat, then leave for 1 hour.

Heat a wok, add a little vegetable oil and stir-fry the ginger so that it is cooked but not brown, then remove from the wok and put to one side.

Heat some more oil in the wok until very hot, then stir-fry the meat in small batches until cooked rare - about 1 minute for each batch. Save the marinade.

Remove the meat from the wok and put to one side. Wipe out the wok, then add the marinade and chicken stock and heat until the mixture begins to boil.

Return the beef and ginger to the wok, add the spinach and stir-fry quickly until the spinach has wilted. Serve at once.
