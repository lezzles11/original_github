CRÊPES

Difficulty: Easy Servings: About 20 Source: Frank Dangereux

Ingredients

125 g flour

20 g sugar

pinch salt

2 eggs

300 ml milk

100 ml cream

Directions

Ideally you need a crêpe pan to make this recipe – a flat steel pan of about 15cm diameter, with a tiny rim, to make one crêpe at a time. You can use a small, non-stick frying pan, as long as the cooking area is the right size.

To make the batter, combine all the ingredients in a liquidiser and work until smooth with no lumps. If mixing by hand, first combine the flour, salt and sugar. Beat the eggs and cream together and slowly mix in the dry ingredients until there are no lumps. If necessary, strain the batter to remove all lumps. Leave the mixed batter to rest for at least one hour before making the crêpes.

Use a set heat to make the crêpes – a half-sized gas flame on reduced heat, or a small electric plate on level two. For the first crêpe, melt a small knob of butter to coat the pan, pouring out the excess if necessary. You shouldn’t need to add more butter for the following crêpes. Your ladle should hold exactly the amount of batter needed for each crêpe (enough to coat the bottom of the pan). For a 15cm diameter pan, the ladle should hold about 13 ml batter. When you pour this into the hot pan, do it fast and without hesitation, so that the crêpes are uniformly crispy. Pour the batter into the middle of the pan and immediately swivel the pan so that the mix covers the bottom evenly. Put back on the heat, leave until the edges go brown, then flick the crêpe over with a spatula and brown the other side. They should cook for one minute on each side. If they take longer than this to go brown, turn the heat up slightly. As you continue, the cooking time should reduce to about 30 seconds on each side.

Make all the crêpes beforehand and keep in the fridge. (Or leave out if you’re making them just before the meal.) You can expect to mess up a few, so make double the crêpe mixture if you’re an inexperienced tosser. In any case it’s good to make extra, they keep well in the fridge and you can eat them with anything – for dessert or a snack with sugar, butter, honey, fresh berries, whatever you like.
