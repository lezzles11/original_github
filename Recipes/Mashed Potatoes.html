Mashed Potatoes

★★★

Dad

Prep Time: 20 minutes Cook Time: 25 minutes Servings: Yield: 10 -12 servings Source: gimmesomeoven.com

Ingredients

2 pounds of potatoes (Yukon Gold or russet potatoes are preferred)

- 1/2 cup of heavy cream (or as needed for desired consistency)

- 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter

- Salt to taste

- 1 teaspoon of sugar (optional, can add a subtle flavor balance)

Directions


1. Prepare the Potatoes:
- Peel the potatoes and cut them into even chunks, about 1 to 2 inches in size.
- Put the cut potatoes into a large pot and cover them with cold water. Add a generous pinch of salt.
- Bring the water to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Let the potatoes cook until they are tender and can be pierced easily with a fork, which should take about 15 to 20 minutes.

2. Heat the Cream and Butter:
- While the potatoes are cooking, combine the cream, butter, and sugar (if using) in a small saucepan.
- Heat the mixture over low heat until the butter melts and the cream is warm. Do not let it boil. Once the butter is melted, remove the saucepan from the heat.

3. Drain and Mash the Potatoes:
- Once the potatoes are done, drain them well to remove all the water.
- Return the potatoes to the pot or place them in a large bowl suitable for mashing.
- Mash the potatoes using a potato masher or fork until they reach your preferred consistency.

4. Add Cream and Butter Mixture:
- Gradually add the warm cream and butter mixture to the mashed potatoes, stirring and mashing until the potatoes are smooth and creamy. Add only as much of the mixture as needed to achieve the consistency you like.
- Taste and adjust the seasoning, adding salt as needed.

5. Serve:
- Once your mashed potatoes are smooth and fully seasoned, transfer them to a serving dish.
- Serve immediately while they're hot and enjoy!

#### Tips:

- The key to creamy mashed potatoes is to add the liquid (cream and butter mixture) a little at a time. You may not need to use all of it, depending on the type and moisture content of your potatoes.
- For extra smooth potatoes, you can use a potato ricer or food mill instead of a masher.
- Sugar is entirely optional and is not a typical ingredient in classic mashed potatoes, but some people find that it can enhance the natural sweetness of the potatoes. Use it sparingly.

This simple yet classic mashed potatoes recipe will complement any main dish beautifully!
