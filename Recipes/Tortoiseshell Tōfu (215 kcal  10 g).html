Tortoiseshell Tōfu (215 kcal / 10 g)

Servings: 2 Source: Japanese Cooking; Shizuo Tsuji

Ingredients

2 cups dashi (or light chicken stock)

½ Tbsp sugar

2 Tbsps mirin

2 Tbsps dark soy sauce pinch salt

1 cake tōfu (bean curd), pressed for 1 hour (see this page); do not press “silk” tōfu

3 Tbsps cornstarch, mixed with 3 Tbsps water


kinome sprigs or finely shred-cut fresh ginger

Directions

To cook and serve: Mix the dashi, sugar, mirin, and dark soy sauce in a medium-sized pot and bring to a boil. Turn down to a simmer and gradually add salt, tasting often.

Cut the bean curd cake into quarters and carefully slide them into the pot. Be careful to keep liquid at a simmer—higher heat will break up the bean curd. Ladle the liquid over the top and sides till the bean curd is heated thoroughly, about 4 minutes.

With a pancake turner, remove the bean curd pieces to deep individual dishes (2 pieces per serving). Keep the pot on the heat and add the mixture of water and cornstarch to the simmering liquid. Stir until thickened, about 1 minute. Ladle the thickened sauce over the heated bean curd. Garnish with a sprig of kinome or very finely shred-cut ginger. Serve immediately, and eat with teaspoons instead of chopsticks.
