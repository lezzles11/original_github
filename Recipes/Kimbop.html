Kimbop

Difficulty: Medium Servings: 5 Source: Korean Home Cooking

Ingredients

1 teaspoon grapeseed or olive oil

4 large eggs, beaten

1 bunch spinach or watercress

Kosher salt

1 carrot, cut into ½-inch (12 mm) wide strips

4 cups (368 g) cooked sushi or short-grain rice, still hot

2 tablespoons rice wine vinegar

5 sheets roasted seaweed

½ pound (225 g) yellow pickled radish (see Note), cut into long sticks

½ pound (225 g) crab stick (imitation crab), halved lengthwise

Sesame oil, for brushing

Directions

In a nonstick skillet over low heat, heat the oil. Pour in the eggs so that they cover the bottom of the pan in a thin layer, and let them cook until they are set. Remove the omelet to a cutting board and let it cool to room temperature before cutting it into thick ribbons as shown in the how-to.

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and prepare a mixing bowl with salted ice-cold water. Drop the spinach or watercress into the boiling water and let it cook just until it is wilted and turns bright green, about 7 seconds. Remove the greens from the water with tongs and place them in the ice water. Swish the greens around in the water with your hands until the greens have cooled, then drain them in a colander, squeezing out all the water with your hands.

In a skillet, heat 1 tablespoon water and a pinch of salt over medium heat and cook the carrot just until it begins to soften, about 2 minutes. Remove the carrot from the heat and set aside to cool.

When you’re ready to form the kimbop, put the warm rice in a mixing bowl and mix in the vinegar and 1 teaspoon salt. Let cool slightly before forming the rolls.

Form the rolls according to the directions. Slice the rolls into thick rounds and eat them the day they are made.

HOW-TO KIMBOP

Season the rice when it has cooled to warm. Taste it, and add more vinegar or salt as needed.

Set the roasted seaweed on a bamboo mat, then place about ½ cup (92 g) rice on the bottom half of the sheet.

Set up a small bowl of water and use wet fingers to spread the rice into a thin layer on the bottom two-thirds of the seaweed, dipping your fingers in the water as needed so that the rice doesn’t stick to your hands. You want the rice to cover the roasted seaweed in an even layer.

Lay the cooked greens on the rice in a single layer near the bottom of the roasted seaweed, then place the yellow pickled radish, the carrots, a strip of egg, and the crab stick.

Pick up the edge of the bamboo closest to you and fold it over the fillings.

Use your fingertips to tuck the kimbop up under the rolled edge of the bamboo, ensuring the wrapping is tight.

Hold the leading edge of the bamboo mat out and gently roll the bamboo over the kimbop, using your hands to pinch the kimbop into shape. If the seaweed doesn’t stick together, use a few kernels of warm rice as glue.

Make sure the kimbop is tightly rolled by shaping it with the bamboo mat.

Release the kimbop by gently rolling it off the mat.

The kimbop should be perfectly sealed into shape. Prepare a small bowl with sesame oil. Coat your clean hands with the oil and gently rub the outside of the roasted seaweed. This prevents your roll from puckering and losing its shape, and it also adds flavor.
