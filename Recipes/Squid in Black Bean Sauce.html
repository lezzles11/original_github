Squid in Black Bean Sauce

Difficulty: Easy Source: Keith Floyd

Ingredients

350 g/12 oz squid tubes, cleaned

1 green pepper and 1 red pepper, each deseeded and pith removed, cut into 2.5 cm/1 inch squares

1 garlic clove, peeled and finely chopped

4 or 5 spring onions, trimmed and finely chopped

1 cm /½ inch piece fresh root ginger, peeled and finely chopped

1 tablespoon rice wine, sake or dry sherry

1 tablespoon black bean sauce

sea salt, to taste

vegetable oil, for stir-frying

a few drops of sesame oil, to finish

Directions

Cut open each squid tube lengthways to form a flat sheet and score the inside of the squid in a criss-cross pattern, then cut into 3.5 cm/1½ inch squares.

Fill a pan with lightly salted water and bring to the boil. Drop in the squid and blanch for about 1 minute. Remove the squid, drain and dry on kitchen paper.

Next, heat a wok, add a little vegetable oil and stir-fry the peppers for a minute or two.

Add the garlic, spring onions, ginger, salt and squid and continue to stir-fry for another 2 minutes.

Add the rice wine, sake or dry sherry and the black bean sauce and stir until warmed through.

Transfer the whole lot to a warm serving dish, add a few drops of sesame oil and serve hot.
