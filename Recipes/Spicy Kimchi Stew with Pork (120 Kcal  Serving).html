Spicy Kimchi Stew with Pork (120 Kcal / Serving)

★★★★

Lesley

Difficulty: Easy Servings: 8 Source: Culinary Institute of America

Ingredients

10 oz/284 g large-dice onions

1 fl oz/30 mL vegetable oil

2 garlic cloves, sliced

1 lb 8 oz/680 g thinly sliced pork shoulder

1 fl oz/30 mL gochujang (Korean hot pepper paste)

2 tsp/2 g dried ground Korean chile

1 lb 8 oz/680 g cabbage kimchi, cut into bite-size pieces

1½ qt/1.44 L white chicken or beef stock

1 lb/454 g large-dice soft tofu

Salt, as needed

2 green onions, sliced

1 jalapeño, thinly sliced

Directions

In a rondeau or similar pot over medium heat, sweat the onions in the oil until softened, for about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and continue to sweat until fragrant, about 30 seconds.

Add the pork and cook until the meat turns opaque. Add the gochujang and ground Korean chile and cook for another 30 seconds.

Add the kimchi and just enough white stock to barely submerge. Bring to a boil, and simmer gently for 20 minutes.

Add the tofu, stirring very carefully in order not to break it apart. Continue to simmer very gently for 10 minutes, until the meat is tender. Adjust seasoning as necessary.

To serve, ladle the stew into a deep bowl and garnish with sliced green onions and jalapeño.

Notes

Used 1000 dashi instead of chicken stock
