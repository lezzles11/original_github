Banana Souffle (150 Kcal / Serving)

★★★★

Lesley

Difficulty: Easy Servings: 4 Source: Pier

Ingredients

SOUFFLÉ

unsalted butter, softened, for greasing

granulated sugar, for coating

3–4 ripe bananas

2 tablespoons honey

8 egg whites from 55 g (2 oz) eggs

4 of 2 tsp caster (superfine) sugar

icing (confectioners’) sugar, for dusting

chantilly cream

Directions

Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F/Gas 6).

Using a pastry brush, grease the bottom and sides of soufflé ramekins with the butter (they should be lightly, but evenly, coated). Coat the inside of the ramekins with the sugar (this will help the soufflé to rise and give it a nice sugary crust).

Cut the banana into pieces and place in a blender. Add the honey, blend on the highest speed until the mixture forms a smooth paste, then spoon it into a bowl. Place the egg whites into another bowl and whisk until just before soft peaks form. Sprinkle the sugar into the whites and whisk until the whites form soft peaks (take care, as the egg whites don’t require as firm a peak as for other soufflés, otherwise the soufflé will be too dry). Take one-third of the whites and whisk into the banana base to obtain a smooth mixture. With a spatula, quickly but lightly fold in the remaining egg whites until completely folded into the base.

Spoon the soufflé mix into the prepared ramekins and give each one a few taps on the bench to release any air bubbles. Using a palette knife, scrape off any excess soufflé mix from the top of the ramekin, and wipe around the inside top of the ramekins to ensure the soufflé doesn’t stick to the mould and will therefore rise. Place the ramekins on a tray with some space between each one and bake in the oven for 7-8 minutes (the soufflés should be soft and not fully cooked in the centre). Dust with icing sugar and serve immediately with the chantilly cream.
