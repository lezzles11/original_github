Crispy Baguette Rounds with Rocket, Red Onion, Tomato and Anchovy

Dad To Try

Difficulty: Medium Servings: 8 Source: app.ckbk.com

Ingredients

2 baguettes

3 ripe tomatoes, peeled and with seeds removed

1 red onion, peeled

1 small bunch wild rocket

16 anchovy fillets

olive oil to drizzle

juice of one lemon to drizzle

salt and pepper

Directions

Once you’ve peeled and seeded the tomatoes, chop them very finely. Also finely chop the red onion. Note that when I say chop finely, I mean crush into tiny pieces.

Mix the tomato and onion together. Do NOT add seasoning yet, as salt draws water from tomatoes. If you like, add a little olive oil to the mixture to make it smooth.

Cut the baguettes into rounds approximately 2cm thick. Cut straight down through the loaf, not at an angle, as you don’t want the slices to be too large. Place the bread rounds on a baking tray, drizzle with olive oil and brown both sides under the grill. Let them become very crispy – the crust should splinter when you bite it.

Take them out and arrange them on your serving dish or platter. Add salt and pepper to the tomato/onion mix. Using a teaspoon, spread a layer of tomato/onion mixture on each toasted round.

Wash and coarsely chop the wild rocket, and sprinkle it on the rounds. Cut the anchovy fillets in half lengthways, and top each toast round with half a fillet.

Squeeze a few drops of lemon juice and drizzle a little olive oil over the whole lot, and there you have a crispy, tasty snack to amuse the palate. If you like, spread olive tapenade on the crusts before the tomato/onion mixture, instead of or as well as anchovies.
