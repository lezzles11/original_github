Stir-Fried Chicken with Crispy Basil

Difficulty: Easy Source: app.ckbk.com

Ingredients

500 g/1 lb 2 oz skinless chicken breast fillets, cut into 2.5 cm/1 inch strips

2 handfuls of fresh basil leaves

2 tablespoons chicken stock

½ teaspoon Demerara or palm sugar

1 red pepper, deseeded and cut into strips

1 red onion, peeled, cut in half and sliced quite thickly

vegetable oil, for deep-frying

FOR THE MARINADE:

4 garlic cloves, peeled and finely chopped

4 small, dried red bird’s eye chillies, crushed

2 tablespoons oyster sauce

1 tablespoon Thai fish sauce (nam pla)

Directions

Mix all the ingredients for the marinade in a bowl. Add the chicken strips and mix well, then cover and leave in the fridge for about 30 minutes.

Heat at least 5 cm/2 inches of oil in a wok or heavy-based frying pan. Drop in two-thirds of the basil leaves and fry for about 30 seconds until they are crispy. Using a slotted spoon, remove from the wok and drain on kitchen paper, then set aside.

Throw this oil away, heat a small amount of fresh oil in the wok and stir-fry half the chicken over a high heat for 4–5 minutes, then remove and drain on kitchen paper. Stir-fry the remaining chicken, then return all the chicken to the wok.

Add the stock and sugar to the wok, then the red pepper and onion and stir-fry for another couple of minutes.

Stir in the fresh basil leaves, turn into a serving dish and garnish with the crispy basil leaves.
