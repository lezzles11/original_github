Charred Eggplant with White Soy and Chiles

Difficulty: Easy Servings: 2 to 4 Source: Korean Home Cooking

Ingredients

1 pound (455 kg) Japanese eggplant

Vegetable oil

2 scallions, halved lengthwise and then thinly sliced

2 teaspoons minced garlic

2 teaspoons toasted sesame seeds

2 teaspoons white soy sauce (see Note)

1 teaspoon fish sauce

2 teaspoons sesame oil

1 tablespoon thinly sliced Holland chile, seeds removed

Directions

Cut the eggplant into 2-inch (5 cm) pieces, then cut those in half lengthwise. (Leave the skin on.)

Lightly coat a griddle or cast-iron skillet with vegetable oil, then heat the pan over high heat.

Place the eggplant pieces cut side down on the griddle or skillet—working in batches if you need to—and let them cook undisturbed until the cut side is charred, about 2 minutes. Watch your heat—you want the eggplants to char, but you don’t want them to burn.

Flip the eggplant pieces over and let the other side brown slightly, about a minute, and then lower the heat slightly and let them cook until they are just tender and cooked through, another 2 minutes. (They’re done when they feel soft if you squeeze them gently with a pair of tongs.)

Remove the eggplant pieces to a mixing bowl and let them cool until you can handle them with your fingers. Shred each piece lengthwise into 2 to 3 strips each.

Add the scallions, garlic, sesame seeds, soy sauce, fish sauce, sesame oil, and chiles to the bowl, then mix everything together well with your hands. Adjust seasoning to taste.

Serve at room temperature or cold. It lasts for about 2 to 3 days in the refrigerator.
